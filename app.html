Metro BaSau — User Guide

Welcome to Metro BaSau Map Builder!

Metro BaSau is a professional, high-performance editor that allows you to construct and analyze complex transit maps. Whether you are building real-world network maps or abstract designs, these instructions will get you started quickly.

Drawing Tracks & Stations

  • Select a Color: Choose a color from the Line Color palette in the left sidebar or press keys 1 to 8.
  • Draw Tracks: Select a track type or the Auto Tool (which resolves curves/crossings automatically). Hold down the left mouse button on the grid and drag to draw a line. Release to commit the track.
  • Create a Station: Select the Station tool (represented by a white circle icon). Click on any grid cell to place a station. You will be prompted to enter a station name. You can place stations on top of existing tracks to make them stops.

Editing & Erasing

  • Erase Elements: Select the Eraser tool (trash can icon or press E). Click and drag over cells to remove tracks and stations.
  • Undo/Redo Actions: Use Ctrl + Z to undo and Ctrl + Y to redo your changes at any time.
  • Pan & Zoom: Select the Pan tool (hand icon or press V) to click and drag to scroll the canvas. Use your mouse scroll wheel to zoom in and out.

Advanced Features

1. Transfer Connections

Create transfer pathways between adjacent stations (especially on different lines) to allow passengers to transition. Choose the Transfer tool, then click the start station and then the destination station.

2. One-Way Tracks

Control the direction of flow on line tracks. Choose the One-Way tool and click on a placed track to assign an operating direction. Repeated clicks cycle through available directions.

3. Route Finder

Select a departure station in From and an arrival station in To. Click Find Route to see the path, distance, number of stations, and transition line instructions. You can also exclude specific lines or generate a random route.

4. Reference Image

Load an image file as a background blueprint underlay. Use the opacity and scale sliders to calibrate the drawing size, and click Move Reference (or hold Shift) to drag the blueprint around.
